Projects

Research projects and systems

This page highlights the artifacts and system-building layer behind the papers: benchmarks, method lines, and workflow-oriented research tools.

I do not think of research output as papers alone. The more interesting question is often what infrastructure, benchmark, workflow, or evaluation layer had to exist for the research to become convincing in the first place.

IdeaBench

Benchmarking infrastructure · 2024–2025

A benchmark and evaluation layer for studying how well large language models generate research ideas rather than merely polished prose.

  • Designed to support comparison across models, prompting strategies, and inference-time methods.
  • Represents my interest in making scientific ideation measurable, inspectable, and reproducible.

Inference-time learning for ideation

Method line · 2025–2026

A line of work on improving model-generated research ideas through inference-time adversarial learning and reinforcement learning.

  • Includes the InfAL and InfRL directions.
  • Focuses on controllable post-training improvement rather than relying only on scaling larger models.

LLMs for scientific discovery

Research workflow systems · 2024–Present

An ongoing research agenda on grounding foundation models in scientific workflows, literature use, evaluation, and hypothesis generation.

  • Connects idea generation, critique, optimization, and knowledge use.
  • Reflects a broader systems view of how model outputs should fit into real research practice.

Graph robustness and long-range dependency modeling

Graph ML · 2022–2023

Earlier work on robust graph neural networks under adversarial structural attacks and across different homophily regimes.

  • Includes the GalNN direction described in the CV.
